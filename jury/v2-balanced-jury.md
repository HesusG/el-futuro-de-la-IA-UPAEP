### Points of Consensus
- C1 and C2 converged to unanimous 8 — solid but not exceptional
- The personal voice ("el docente que aprende a preguntar") is the entry's strongest asset
- C3 is the weakest dimension — Google Search as evidence demo doesn't constitute rigorous argumentation
- Andrés valued the voice highest (C5=9); Sofía valued it lowest (C5 originally 7, revised to 8)

### Strengths
- Most authentic personal voice of any version
- The dual-role tension (teacher AND student) is genuine and well-deployed
- Conclusion is memorable: "La mejor versión del docente del futuro no es quien domina la IA, sino quien sabe hacer las preguntas correctas"

### Weaknesses
- Evidence layer is thin — Google Search demo ≠ rigorous argumentation
- Competency framework (P4 Canvas) needs measurable indicators
- No structural barriers addressed (infrastructure, salaries, institutional resistance)

### Phase 2 Questions
1. What empirical evidence supports "the teacher who learns to ask" producing better outcomes?
2. How does this position vs. Selwyn's techno-solutionism critique?
3. Is the "Dra. Valeria" voice authentically sustained or does it become artificial?
4. Does the Canvas competency framework include measurable indicators?
5. What specific AI tools are recommended beyond the metaphor of "learning to ask"?

### Recommendation
**Backup option.** Strong personal voice but weak on evidence and creativity. Would place in upper-middle tier but unlikely to win.
