### Points of Consensus

### Phase 2 Challenge Questions
1. Can you name one idea that emerged from the dialogue that didn't exist in the prior literature?
2. Was there a moment where AI genuinely changed your thinking, or was it always executing your score?
3. How did you verify the data underlying the Python charts?
4. If we remove all external authority references, what remains of YOUR argument?
5. How will you adapt the conclusion if Gemini produces responses misaligned with the "redesign" thesis?
6. What's Plan B if Canvas doesn't activate or code execution fails?
7. Do the 6 LatAm countries' data exist homogeneously for all 5 radar dimensions?
8. Is the title "Rediseñar, no reemplazar" a thesis or a slogan?

### Agent Verdicts

**J1 Dra. Marcela:** The most deliberate argumentative architecture in the set, with a 5-act arc that demonstrates intellectual mastery. Its vulnerability is execution fragility — if one feature fails, the domino chain breaks.

**J2 Prof. Andrés:** Converts 5 prompts into a composed argument with genuine intellectual stakes. The FCL-zones hypothesis is an original contribution. Missing: a moment of genuine vulnerability or surprise.

**J3 Ing. Sofía:** The most sophisticated prompt engineering design. Every Gemini capability has a defensible purpose. Risk: the P4 data visualization depends on unverifiable AI-generated data.

**J4 Diego:** Technically impressive, intellectually ambitious, but choreographed. The Gem pre-configuration scripts the dialogue before it starts. Would ask: what would you have discovered without the scaffolding?

### Recommendation
**Strong candidate for submission.** Highest average score. Best balance of rigor, creativity, and structure. Main risk is execution — must pre-test all 5 features and have fallback plans documented.
