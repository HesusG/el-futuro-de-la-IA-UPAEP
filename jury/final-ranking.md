## The Top 3 — Detailed Comparison

### v3-creative (43.25) — "The Architect"
- **Strengths:** Best overall balance. 5-act arc is structurally unmatched. Full feature showcase with purpose. Meta-critical P5 is the strongest single prompt moment.
- **Weaknesses:** Choreographed — Diego's persistent concern about authenticity. The Gem pre-configuration debate never fully resolved. Data in P4 charts may be unverifiable.
- **Execution risk:** MEDIUM. 5 sequential features. Has documented fallbacks.

### v8-fresh-bold (≈43.0) — "The Tribunal"
- **Strengths:** Highest C3 creativity in R1 (9.25). Five witnesses from five countries create emotional + intellectual breadth. "La IA no mató nada. Llegó a un velorio." Conditional verdict is smarter than any clean conclusion. Completely original references (no UNESCO overlap).
- **Weaknesses:** Sofía flags C5 risk — prompts are "stage directions" not personal writing. Gemini must sustain 3 simultaneous voices. Conservative judges might find the format disrespectful.
- **Execution risk:** HIGH. Gemini voice-sustaining. But the payoff is enormous.

### v4-bold (42.25) — "The Decolonialist"
- **Strengths:** HIGHEST creativity score anywhere (9.50). P2's first-person AI confrontation is unprecedented. The "can't fail" design — even a corporate disclaimer becomes evidence. Semiotic color palette. Freire closing.
- **Weaknesses:** Dependency chain — weak P1 cascades. Jury unfamiliar with decolonial theory might read P2 as antagonism. P4 needs pre-selected data.
- **Execution risk:** HIGH. But P2 insurance policy is brilliant.

## Cross-Cutting Insights from the Jury Process

### What separates the top from the bottom
1. **Creativity is the differentiator.** C3 scores explain 90% of the ranking variance. v1 scored 4.25 on creativity; v4 scored 9.50. Everything else is noise.
2. **Critical interaction requires REACTIVE dialogue.** Entries that instruct AI (v1, v2) score lower on C1 than entries that confront, redirect, or challenge AI responses (v4, v6, v7).
3. **Personal voice matters more than institutional voice.** v2's authentic teacher voice (C5=8.25) outperformed v1's polished bureaucratic voice (C5=8.00) despite similar polish levels.
4. **Execution risk is tolerable if the design accounts for it.** v3's documented fallbacks justify its ambitious feature use. v6's lack of fallbacks is its Achilles heel.

### The single most important finding
**Every version was penalized for the same thing: not showing genuine surprise or discovery.** All four agents, across all versions, asked some version of: "Was there a moment where the AI changed your thinking?" No version convincingly demonstrated emergent insight. The winner will be the entry where the participant's conclusion feels CHANGED by the conversation, not CONFIRMED.
