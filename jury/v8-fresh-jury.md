### Best Level: BOLD ("El Tribunal de Quito") — 3 of 4 votes
- Sofía recommended Balanced for defensibility, but Bold won on ambition

### Points of Consensus (zero spread)
- **C2 = 9.00** — The tribunal form is inherently pedagogical (forces perspective-taking, evidence, ethical reasoning)
- **C4 = 9.00** — The 5-witness structure provides a clear architectural skeleton; writer's block is nearly impossible

### The C5 Problem (persistent 7.25 across Rounds 2-5)
The student disappears behind the characters. The prompts read as "stage directions" not personal writing. The contest evaluates the PARTICIPANT's writing, not the AI's literary output.

**Recommended fixes:**
1. Make the student the narrator-judge who delivers the verdict from personal experience
2. Reduce from 5 witnesses to 3 (more depth per voice, more room for student)
3. Add a mandatory "break the fourth wall" personal paragraph
4. Embed one linguistic-bias testimony from the Balanced level

### Score Trajectory (R1 → R5)
- C3 dropped from 9.25 → 8.50 (Diego's dilution argument: 5 voices in ~1500 words = shallow)
- C2 rose from 8.50 → 9.00 (Andrés's argument: the form IS the pedagogy)
- C5 dropped from 8.00 → 7.25 and locked there

### Phase 2 Questions
1. How do we rewrite Bold so the student is unmistakably the narrator (not the playwright)?
2. Should we reduce from 5 testimonies to 3 for depth within word limits?
3. Should students subvert the tribunal form (AI cross-examines? ambiguous verdict?) for originality?
4. Can we graft Balanced's linguistic-bias angle into Bold (one witness testifies about language)?
5. Should there be a mandatory "personal paragraph" breaking the fourth wall?

### Agent Verdicts

**J1 Dra. Marcela:** Mature family with clear quality gradient. Bold is extraordinary but execution risk is high — Gemini must sustain 3 voices across 5 prompts.

**J2 Prof. Andrés:** Strongest creative architecture evaluated. The five testimonies function as a novel-in-miniature. Nobody else will have a Zapotec-speaking witness or a philosophy student from Havana.

**J3 Ing. Sofía:** Balanced is the most intellectually valuable (linguistic bias = real, under-discussed problem). Bold is spectacular as theater but depends on Gemini's performance more than any other version.

**J4 Diego:** Bold is a competition-winner. It reframes the contest question through five human stories. The conditional verdict is smarter than any clean conclusion. "Fine" doesn't win contests.

### Recommendation
**Top-3 contender (submit Bold).** C5 weakness is fixable with prompt modifications. The tribunal format gives the jury something they've never seen before. If literary resonance matters, this could win. Address the voice problem before execution.
